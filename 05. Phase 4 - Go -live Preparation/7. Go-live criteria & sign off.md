# 7. Go-Live Criteria & Sign-Off

## 7.1 Objective
Define the mandatory conditions that must be met before the IAM solution is allowed to transition into production.  
These criteria ensure security, operational continuity, data accuracy, and end-user readiness.

## 7.2 Go-Live Criteria

### 1. Technical Readiness
- All Entra ID configurations deployed and validated in pre-production  
- Conditional Access policies tested with no critical impact  
- Identity Governance (Access Packages, Lifecycle Workflows, Access Reviews) configured and validated  
- Provisioning flows (HRIS → Entra ID → AD → applications) tested successfully  
- Sentinel monitoring and alert rules operational  

### 2. Data Readiness
- HRIS data validated with no critical attribute gaps  
- Role/entitlement mappings finalized  
- No major data quality issues outstanding  
- All disabled/terminated accounts reconciled  

### 3. Operational Readiness
- Incident response playbook approved  
- Cutover plan validated by all involved teams  
- Support model and on-call schedule confirmed  
- Hypercare plan finalized  

### 4. Security & Compliance Readiness
- Security policies validated by IAM + CISO  
- SoD (Segregation of Duties) reviewed  
- Audit requirements documented and met  
- Logging and retention validated in Sentinel  

### 5. Business Readiness
- Communication plan approved and scheduled  
- End-user training completed for targeted personas  
- Stakeholders confirm that operational impact is acceptable  

## 7.3 Go-Live Sign-Off

### Required Approvals
- IAM Lead  
- Security Office (CISO / ISO)  
- HRIS / Application Owners  
- Infrastructure Lead  
- Program Manager / Portfolio Owner  

### Go/No-Go Meeting Output
- Go decision with expected cutover start  
- No-Go decision + blocking issues list  
- Conditional Go with required mitigations  

## 7.4 Deliverables
- Go-Live readiness checklist (signed)  
- Go/No-Go meeting minutes  
- Stakeholder approval record
