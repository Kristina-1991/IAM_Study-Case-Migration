# 7. Governance Ownership Model (RACI)

## Purpose
This section defines the governance ownership model required to operate the future IAM ecosystem.  
The RACI clarifies *who is responsible for what*, reduces friction between business and IT, and establishes a sustainable operational model across the identity lifecycle.

The model is designed around five core IAM functional areas:
1. Identity Lifecycle Management  
2. Access Provisioning & Role Governance  
3. Privileged Access Governance  
4. Access Reviews & Compliance  
5. Monitoring & Incident Response  

---

## 7.1 Governance Principles

- **Clear Accountability:** Each control has a single accountable owner.  
- **Business-driven Access:** Business units own access decisions; IT executes.  
- **Least Privilege by Design:** Role and access models prevent privilege accumulation.  
- **Shared Responsibility Model:** Aligned with NIS2 and ISO expectations.  
- **Auditability:** Every action must be evidence-based and traceable.

## 7.3 Operating Model Roles

### **IAM Center of Excellence (CoE)**
- Owns IAM roadmap and architecture  
- Maintains standards, policies, and the role model  
- Coordinates with Security, IT Ops, and Business Units  

### **Business Application Owners**
- Make access decisions  
- Validate roles, permissions, and certifications  
- Ensure SoD compliance for their applications  

### **Security / SOC**
- Monitors identity threats  
- Handles incidents and escalations  
- Oversees compliance and audit readiness  

### **HR / Workday**
- Manages authoritative source data quality  
- Triggers JML events  
- Maintains employment/contractor records

## 7.5 Summary
The governance ownership model ensures clear accountability, business-driven access decisions, and security oversight aligned with regulatory expectations.  
This structure forms the foundation for reliable IAM operations and supports long-term maturity.
